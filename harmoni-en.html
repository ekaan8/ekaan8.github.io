harmonium

28 june 2022
back in history class, i had scribbled some poems on the cover of my textbook. at the time, i was reading about caesar — and just before that, i had read about magellan. i think they inspired me. what i wrote wasn’t very long, but i still believe it might be worth reading, because in my own way, i tried to create a sense of harmony.

bastille

1789 GALIA
the gloomy land of gaul was burning bright,
walls thundered like the sky in might.
the bastille’s rotten roots decayed,
with wicked spirits set to fade.
the dawn of human rights arose,
into dictators’ hearts it rose,
grasping freedom’s banner high,
like waves that crash against the sky.
citizens surged with sharp power’s flight,
shattering dogmas cold as night.
the ocean’s storm, untamed and free,
crushed the jesters of royalty.
here lies paris, there’s distant nantes,
shivering with revolution’s dance.
justice, long drowned by darkened days,
now rises sharp, its sword ablaze—
to burn versailles in fiery haze.

hispañiola, from old world to new world


Columbia
crystal waves were cutting through the sea,
hispañola’s great ships sailed free.
foam scattered wide, behind lay hispañola’s shore,
ships glided forth to horizons never seen before.
memories drifted in the ocean of time,
night sky’s calm stars sent rays that shine,
endlessly flowing to the dark waters deep,
a silent current where shadows creep.


Germania
german lands stretch far beyond the sight,
dark forests and coldness steal the light.
whirlpools of fear awaken in the heart,
frozen souls of the north set them apart.
blond giants ride with barbarian steeds,
the sun never shines on their marshy deeds.
in single file, behind witches they glide,
spreading barbaric laws far and wide.
bodies of iron, hands of bronze so cold,
bound not by laws of warmer worlds told.
cold and dark is what holds them tight,
giving the urge to move and fight.
this is germania — all that they are,
a land where the light feels cold and far.
