on the trail of truth

just as in ancient times, i believe that even in this new age, enlightenment will come through the knowledge of truth — no matter how difficult the path may be. this era, where information is produced and consumed rapidly without true absorption, seems to lack the mystical and enchanting scent of ages past. yet today, we are closer to the stars than ever, and our presence is felt more vividly on ancient planets visited by solar travelers. some things have changed, but some of our traits remain constant: the stars are still the sky’s adornments, its gentle pearls. the allure of enlightenment — of grasping the glow of truth — lies in its unpredictability. one might continue the search in rooms lined with books, or travel far to the east, and still fail to find truth. yet in contrast, watching a sparrow quietly drink from a fountain in a moment of stillness might suddenly reveal it. in that instant, truth becomes a divine gift — though we must remember, even to reach that moment, a person has already journeyed far and wide.
