website

emrekaan.com

this site was designed as a personal portfolio. before i began, i set a few clear principles for myself. every decision was not just about aesthetics, but about shaping the overall experience. the menu is transparent, subtly reflecting the background content. the corners of the boxes are soft. i chose san francisco as the typeface — clean, readable, and free from unnecessary detail. every clickable element is clearly recognizable; hover feedback is noticeable yet understated. from the very beginning, the site has included both light and dark modes, each with its own character. headings, texts, and containers adapt distinctly between day and night views. smooth and intuitive navigation across the site was a key priority. the menu structure is simple, content is accessible, and both language and theme can be switched instantly. although the css for mobile and desktop layouts is entirely different, the navigation logic remains the same. no matter the device, the experience feels familiar.

i paid special attention to how the content adapts automatically when the window is resized, especially on desktop. the way headings, images, and containers adjust to different widths keeps the layout consistent under all conditions. for me, this flexibility is part of visual harmony. technically, the html and script parts brought some challenges, but my main focus was always on css. i spent time thinking about color balance, the feel of spacing, font weights, shadow intensity — all the little details that shape the language of the site. soft transitions, gradient headers, subtle shadows, glassy reflections, fluid text and visuals — they’re all noticeable as you move through the page. i first designed the structure in my mind, knowing what kind of experience i wanted to build. then, through code, i gradually brought it to life. the result is a simple yet intentional piece of work that reflects my approach to design.

instagram @technovision101

this is where technovision lives. in just 10 frames, i explore works, products, and remarkable ideas — especially those born from the world of computer science. there’s also another section called “icontech,” where we remember the visionary minds who built this world — von neumann, ada, shannon, bush, and many others. looking at my posts from past to present, it’s almost a design journey of my own. at first, there were barely any boundaries, and the layout followed a straightforward line. eventually, i grew tired of that and slowly started to discover my own visual language. as i moved closer to the present, i began shaping softer corners, glassy containers, calm gradient tones, and shadows that felt right for the content.

in short, i finally arrived at a style that doesn’t tire the eye and gently invites exploration. this section matters a lot to me — it’s a vast and dynamic world that fascinates me, and here, i get to reflect it entirely through my own taste.

dot-based image

i developed a web application that transforms your visuals into dot-based artworks. with this tool, you can convert your photos into pointillistic-style images and adjust the number and size of the dots to achieve highly detailed results closer to the original. on the other hand, lowering these values creates a simpler, more nostalgic visual atmosphere.

i added 12 distinct color palettes: original, retro, pastel, dark, warm, cool, earth, punk, sunset, ocean, galaxy, and gold. you can download or copy the transformed images as you like.

marcia

there was a time when i was immersed in homer’s iliad and odyssey, reading aristophanes and shakespeare alongside them. i became so familiar with the elevated language of the age of heroes that inspiration found me when i least expected it. i couldn’t stop myself — i began writing down the words and verses that came to mind without pause. what emerged was the opening of a short play set between sea and desert. it’s still unfinished, but has already grown beyond 800 lines.
these verses tell the story of marcia, daughter of the king of crete, her father king balehund, and kefaric — the wild ruler of numidia, across the sea. the tale begins under a lunar eclipse.
to me, the desert is a different expression of the sea — this story reflects a blend of ideas i've long carried. at its core, though, it centers on the conflict between the age of gold and the age of iron, as seen through marcia and kefaric. much like the iliad, it’s a story of east and west.
