our belief in art

art is created not to dominate, but to discover. in our understanding of art, it’s not sharp lines or harsh edges that take center stage, but rather soft transitions, calm textures, and transparent appearances. in the beginning, the pursuit of flawless design and aesthetic peaks defined clear, visible boundaries — like the borders between nations. transitions from one area to another brought abrupt and noticeable changes. the colors were bright, yet lacked vitality. people seemed to be in full control of their devices, yet the atmosphere felt cold and frozen. over time, it began to seem as though everything was just a collection of pre-fabricated objects, shaped solely by technique. as time passed, a freer-spirited belief emerged in response to this view. harsh lines gave way to gentle transitions and quiet curves. freeing ourselves from frozen visuals welcomed the warmth of golden proportions. transparent windows, soft boxes, and subtle bars started to appear. boundaries and curves, no longer clearly defined to the eye, didn’t immediately evoke a desire to possess. yet these forms held hidden sources of inspiration. when perfect design and technique stepped aside for self-discovery, stunning works began to emerge. eventually, these creations grew so captivating that they invited exploration. rigid and frozen appearances gave way to more relaxed and natural feelings, replacing the urge for temporary control.

golden ratio = 1,618

our principle embraces an artistic understanding that moves away from minimalism illuminated by simplicity. we avoid sharp transitions, lines, and corners. we don’t believe they offer pleasing proportions or harmonious flow; their abrupt boundaries strain the eye. instead, we take true delight in simple yet vivid colors, like the gradual shifts in a sunset. our eyes don’t tire — they explore the transitions and boundaries. true dynamism and vitality come after personal discoveries. we care deeply about all of this — it’s never just a matter of simple tasks. creating harmonious, stunning results only happens when technique is absorbed by art. focusing solely on technique and trying to squeeze beauty and aesthetics into it is nothing more than an empty struggle for control. instead, we believe in first creating a work of art — imagining and crafting simple yet striking outcomes that follow one another in perfect proportion — and only then building the technique to serve it. our eyes love simplicity. this affection includes the desert and the sea. we also love places without boundaries, like forests — places ready to be discovered or lost in. but we despise staying within four walls or using elevators; they keep us away from places to explore or from reaching a deeper tranquility.


this is the essence of our principles: to avoid what the eye avoids, and to build what it loves to discover. that’s how remarkable things come to life — through solid vision and deep emotion behind them. our art is a journey of discovery beyond control. with soft transitions and calm textures, we move away from frozen visuals and embrace the warmth of golden proportions. true dynamism and vitality follow personal discoveries. blending technique into art reveals not just aesthetics, but the beauty hidden deep within the soul. that's why we create works that are simple yet striking — because we believe in the power of discovery and feeling.
